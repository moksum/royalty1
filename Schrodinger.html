Erwin Schrodinger

Wave Mechanics

Erwin Schrodinger thought Heisenberg's matrix mechanics unworkable. Drawing inspiration from deBroglie's matter waves, Schrodinger developed an equation for a wave that described the quantum aspects of the system. This collapsable wave has since mired quantum theory with philosophic questions. A quantum entity is not "real" until it is measured?

The Copenhagen Interpretation (CHI) of quantum theory, tied together matrix mechanics, wave mechanics, the uncertainty principle, and complementarity (wave and particle behavior mutually exclude each other, but both are necessary). Einstein and Bohr were at odds over quantum theory for the next 30 years.
