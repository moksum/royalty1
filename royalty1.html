ELECTRONS and PHOTONS

THE 5TH SOLVAY CONFERENCE






Wasn't just a famous photograph....

The photograph of the physicists who participated in the 5th Solvay Conference has been described as the most intelligent gathering of people in history. 17 of the 29 individuals were recipients of the Nobel Prize, and included such household names as Einstein, Bohr, Curie, Planck, and Heisenberg. So striking was the richness of the company that the photo has been equated with an anachronistic gathering of Archimedes, Kepler, Newton, and Galileo.

Often overshadowed by the photo, is the profound impact of the few papers that were actually presented. The equations which predict the outcome of quantum mechanical processes are all uncontroversial. However the underlying explanations of that outcome, interpretations of quantum theory, are still very controversial. The 3 speakers framed to the right--in less than 2 days-- provided the substance for 100 years of that controversy.

1927 conference schedule

Monday 24 October: W.L. Bragg's report

Tuesday 25 October, started 9:00 a.m.: reception.

Tuesday 25 October, afternoon: L. deBroglie's report

Wednesday 26 October, morning: W.Heisenberg's report

Wednesday 26 October, afternoon: E. Schrodinger's report

Thursday 27 October, morning: general discussion (Bohr's contribution)

Friday 28 October, trip to Paris for procession at the Sorbonne.

100 Year Aftermath

The 5th Solvay Conference was held in Brussels, Belgium, 24-29 October 1927
