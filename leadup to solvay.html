Leadup to 5th Solvay

Planck-----------Einstein

Bohr------------Einstein

Quantum theory is traced to December 1900. Max Planck started it all when he developed an equation for blackbody radiation. Perplexed himself, the equation only fit the phenomena if the radiative energy was discontinuous, or "quantized". Einstein and Bohr would shortly thereafter contribute, ending the first wave of quantum theory.

Albert Einstein showed in 1905 - with the photoelectric effect- by displacing discrete electrons from metal, light also behaved as a particle; thus, a wave-particle duality debate. Niels Bohr, via hydrogen spectra, developed the quantum theory of atomic structure - incorporating Planck/Einstein concepts.
