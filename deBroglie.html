Louis de Broglie

Pilot Wave Model

Einstein had uncovered a wave/particle duality in his theory of light quanta. Louis deBroglie, working with electrons, determined that this duality was general, applying not just to light but to all of the physical world. The motion of any particle - photon, electron, proton--matter, is associated with the propagation of a wave. deBroglie's predictions were then experimentally confirmed.

An interesting anecdote involving deBroglie is that after his presentation, another physicist, Wolfgang Pauli commented that the theory had strange implications (known as non-locality) for two particle systems. deBroglie abruptly stopped working on the theory. Pilot Waves were independently "rediscovered" in the 1950's by David Bohm.
