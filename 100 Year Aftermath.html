100 Year Aftermath

Niels Bohr--------

-------Albert Einstein

Bohr insisted that in quantum theory an entity did not exist (not real) until measured--and particles were non-local (a particle can influence another particle, instantaneously, at any distance). Einstein argued for reality (the moon still exists even when not observed) and for locality.

Recent work involving Bell's inequality demonstrates that quantum entities are non-local; Bohr was correct, Einstein incorrect. Recent work also suggests that the wave equation does not collapse. Quantum entities are real; Bohr was wrong, Einstein correct.
