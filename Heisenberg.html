Werner Heisenberg

Matrix Mechanics

Attempting to associate atomic quantum numbers and energy states with frequencies and brightness of light spectra, Werner Heisenberg demonstrated that energy states were quantized and stationary in the Bohr atom. With mathematical assistance from Max Born, a functional model of quantum (matrix) mechanics was achieved.

Heisenberg was an amateur mountain-climber and derived inspiration from nature for his physical musings. Heisenberg is also known for his "uncertainty principle" which states that one cannot know with precision the position and motion of a particle at any time.
